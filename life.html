Mammo-Bench: A Large-scale Benchmark Dataset of Mammography Images

Gaurav Bhole, Suba S and Nita Parekh

Breast cancer remains a significant global health concern, and machine learning algorithms and computer-aided detection systems have shown great promise in enhancing the accuracy and efficiency of mammography image analysis. However, there is a critical need for large, benchmark datasets for training deep learning models for breast cancer detection. In this work we developed Mammo-Bench, a large-scale benchmark dataset of mammography images, by collating data from seven well-curated resources. To ensure consistency across images from diverse sources while preserving clinically relevant features, a preprocessing pipeline that includes breast segmentation, pectoral muscle removal, and intelligent cropping is proposed. The dataset consists of 74,436 high-quality mammographic images from 26,500 patients across 8 countries and is one of the largest open-source mammography databases to the best of our knowledge. Our results establish baseline performance and demonstrate Mammo-Bench's utility as a comprehensive resource for developing and evaluating mammography analysis systems.

A Comprehensive Analysis of Long-Read Sequencing Technologies for Structural Variant Detection

Honours Project Report

Structural Variations (SVs) are major contributors to human genetic diversity and disease, including cancer. Long-read sequencing (LRS) technologies, such as Pacific Biosciences (PacBio) and Oxford Nanopore Technologies (ONT), offer great advantages for SV detection due to their ability to span complex genomic regions. This project aims to conduct a comprehensive comparative analysis of various LRS-based SV calling tools across germline, somatic (cancer cell line), and simulated datasets. The study evaluates a range of SV callers to propose a concrete workflow for accurate SV detection, with a particular focus on Copy Number Variants (CNVs) and Complex SVs (CSVs), leveraging existing tools in innovative ways. The ultimate goal is to contribute to a more accurate and comprehensive understanding of SVs in diverse genomic contexts.
