Mammo-Bench

To address the critical need for large, benchmark datasets for training deep learning models for breast cancer detection, we developed Mammo-Bench, a large-scale benchmark dataset of mammography images, by collating data from seven well-curated resources. The dataset consists of 74,436 high-quality mammographic images from 26,500 patients across 8 countries. Our results establish baseline performance and demonstrate Mammo-Bench's utility as a comprehensive resource for developing and evaluating mammography analysis systems.

DFANet

This paper proposes a Difference Fusion Attention Network (DFANet), a novel multi-task learning framework for Semantic Change Detection (SCD) in remote sensing imagery. DFANet employs a dual encoder-decoder architecture with dedicated paths for Land Cover Mapping (LCM) and Change Detection (CD). The Difference Fusion module leverages channel attention mechanisms to highlight meaningful changes while suppressing noise. Our approach achieves mIoU scores of 73.30% and 74.12%, on TERRA-CD and SECOND datasets respectively, outperforming existing methods and establishing a new benchmark for SCD.

HyperCLSA

HyperCLSA (Hypergraph Contrastive Learning with Self-Attention) is a novel deep learning framework for efficient multi-omics integration. It combines hypergraph-based sample encoding, supervised contrastive learning for latent space alignment, and multi-head self-attention for adaptive fusion of omics modalities. Evaluated on The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) Breast Cancer dataset for PAM50 subtype classification, HyperCLSA achieves a significant performance improvement over state-of-the-art baselines, providing a robust framework for molecular characterization of breast cancer.
