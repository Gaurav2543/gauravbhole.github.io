Publications & Research

Mammo-Bench: A Large-scale Benchmark Dataset of Mammography Images

Gaurav Bhole, Suba S and Nita Parekh

Breast cancer remains a significant global health concern... We developed Mammo-Bench, a large-scale benchmark dataset of mammography images, by collating data from seven well-curated resources...

To address the critical need for large, benchmark datasets for training deep learning models for breast cancer detection, we developed Mammo-Bench, a large-scale benchmark dataset of mammography images, by collating data from seven well-curated resources. The dataset consists of 74,436 high-quality mammographic images from 26,500 patients across 8 countries. Our results establish baseline performance and demonstrate Mammo-Bench's utility as a comprehensive resource for developing and evaluating mammography analysis systems.

DFANet: A Difference Fusion Attention-based method for Semantic Change Detection

Omkar Oak, Rukmini Nazre, Rujuta Budke, Suraj Sawant, Gaurav Bhole

Semantic Change Detection (SCD) in remote sensing imagery is critical for monitoring landscape transitions. Existing methods struggle with inefficient feature integration and high computational complexity...

This paper proposes a Difference Fusion Attention Network (DFANet), a novel multi-task learning framework for SCD in remote sensing imagery. DFANet employs a dual encoder-decoder architecture with dedicated paths for Land Cover Mapping (LCM) and Change Detection (CD). The Difference Fusion module leverages channel attention mechanisms to highlight meaningful changes while suppressing noise. Our approach achieves mIoU scores of 73.30% and 74.12%, on TERRA-CD and SECOND datasets respectively, outperforming existing methods and establishing a new benchmark for SCD.

HyperCLSA: A Hypergraph Contrastive Learning Pipeline for Multi-Omics Data Integration

Contribution to project

Accurate molecular subtyping of cancer is crucial for personalized medicine. We propose HyperCLSA (Hypergraph Contrastive Learning with Self-Attention), a novel deep learning framework for efficient multi-omics integration...

HyperCLSA combines hypergraph-based sample encoding, supervised contrastive learning for latent space alignment, and multi-head self-attention for adaptive fusion of omics modalities. Evaluated on The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) Breast Cancer dataset for PAM50 subtype classification, HyperCLSA achieves a significant performance improvement over state-of-the-art baselines, providing a robust framework for molecular characterization of breast cancer.
